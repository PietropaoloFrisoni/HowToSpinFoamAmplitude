Spin foam amplitude¶

  • Dynamics of loop quantum gravity in a background independent and Lorentz covariant way.
  • Transition amplitudes between spin network states at the boundary.
  • Regularized with a triangulation 2-complex $\Delta$
$$ A_{\Delta} = \sum_{j_f, i_e} \prod_f A_f(j_f) \prod_e A_e(i_e) \prod_v A_v \left(j_f, \ i_e\right) $$

2-complex $\Delta$ components¶

Triangulation 2-complex
4-simplex $\rightleftarrows$ vertex
tetrahedron $\rightleftarrows$ edge
triangle $\rightleftarrows$ face

Spin foam amplitude - EPRL-FK¶

  • Edge amplitude and face amplitude $$ A_f(j_f) = 2 j_f + 1 \qquad A_e(i_e) = 2 i_e +1$$
  • Simplicial vertex
  • Lorentzian holonomies at each edge $g \in SL(2,\mathbb{C})$
  • Spins to face $j_f$ and intertwiners $i_e$ to edges
  • Gamma simple irreducible representations of $SL(2,\mathbb{C})$ $$ D^{\gamma j_f ,j_f}_{j_f m j_f n} (g_t^{-1} g_s) $$
  • Magnetic indices contracted with $\{4jm\}$ symbols at edges
$$ A_v \left(j_f, \ i_e\right) = \sum_{m_s,m_t} \int \prod_{e \in v} \mathrm{d} g_e \delta(g_{e'}) \prod_{f \in v} D^{\gamma j_f, j_f}_{j_f m_{t} j_f m_{s}} (g_{t}^{-1} g_{s}) \prod_{e \ni f} \left( \begin{array}{c} j_f\\ m_p \end{array}\right)^{(i_e)} $$

Booster decomposition¶

  • Difficult problem $\to$ divide and conquer
  • Smart parametrization of $SL(2,\mathbb{C})$ (Cartan's) Speziale 2016 $$ g = u e^{- i \tfrac{1}{2} r \sigma_3} v^\dagger \qquad r\in [0,+\infty) , \ u,v \in SU(2)$$
  • Measure and irreps decompose nicely $$ \mathrm{d}g = \dfrac{1}{4\pi} \sinh^2 r \mathrm{d} r \mathrm{d}u \mathrm{d}v \qquad\qquad D^{\gamma j_f, j_f}_{l_f m j_f n} (g) = \sum_p D^{l_f}_{mp} (g)d^{\gamma j_f, j_f}_{l_f j_f p} (r) D^{j_f}_{p n} (g)$$
  • Collect similar group elements together $$ D^{\gamma j_f, j_f}_{j_f m_{t} j_f m_{s}} (g_{t}^{-1} g_{s}) = \sum_{l_f = j_f}^{\infty} \sum_{p_f} D^{\gamma j_f, j_f}_{j_f m_{t} l_f p_f} (g_{t}^{-1})D^{\gamma j_f, j_f}_{l_f p_f j_f m_{s}} (g_{s}) $$
  • Perform all the $SU(2)$ integrals in terms of invariants

Booster decomposed EPRL vertex amplitude¶

Linear combination of $\{15j\}$ symbols $$ A_v \left(j_f, \ i_e\right) = \sum_{l_f=j_f}^\infty \sum_{k_e} \{ 15 j\} (j_f,l_f) \prod_{e = 2}^{5} B_4^\gamma \left( l_f, j_f ; i_{e} ,k_{e} \right) (2k_{e}+1) $$ weighted by the booster functions $$B_4^\gamma\left( j_f, l_f ; i,k\right) ={\small \sum_{ p_f } \left(\begin{array}{cccc} l_1 & l_2 & l_3 & l_4 \\ p_1 & p_2 & p_3 & p_4 \end{array}\right)^{(k)} \left(\int_0^\infty \mathrm{d} r \frac{1}{4\pi}\sinh^2r \, \bigotimes_{f=1}^4 d^{\gamma j_f,j_f}_{l_f j_f p_f}(r) \right) \left(\begin{array}{cccc} j_1 & j_2 & j_3 & j_4 \\ p_1 & p_2 & p_3 & p_4 \end{array}\right)^{(i)}} $$

  • Six sums
  • SU(2) invariants
  • One dimensional integrals (booster functions)

Numerical implementation¶

The frameworks sl2cfoam and sl2cfoam-next

Advantages:

  • Open source (help up make it better)
  • Written in C (speed)
  • Modular and Scalable (any amplitude)
  • Optimized for HPC (many nodes, many cores)
  • User friendly (Julia interactive interface)

Disadvantages

  • Resource demanding (costly)
  • Unavoidable approximation (truncation parameter)

Three main ingredients¶

  1. $SU(2)$ invariants are computed using WIGXJPF and FASTWIGXJ Johansson, Forssn 2015
  2. Booster functions (1D bounded integrals, oscillating adaptive GaussKronrod quadrature method)
  1. Convergent unbounded sums $$ \sum_{l_f = j_f}^\infty \to \sum_{l_f = j_f}^{j_f + \Delta l}$$ Uniform truncation parameter $\Delta l$
  • good approximation (amplitude is finite)
  • how good (weak control on the error)
  • rule of thumb ($\Delta l \gtrsim \mathrm{max}j_f$)
  • convergence acceleration technique (Aitken delta-squared)

Using extrapolation techniques¶

Implement Aitken's delta-squared process $$A_v \approx \frac{A_v(\Delta l)A_v(\Delta l-2)-A_v(\Delta l-1)^2}{A_v(\Delta l)-2 A_v(\Delta l-1) +A_v(\Delta l-2)}$$

In [8]:
function extrapolate(Av_Dl)
    l_id = length(Av_Dl)
    num = Av_Dl[l_id] * Av_Dl[l_id-2] - Av_Dl[l_id-1]^2 
    den = Av_Dl[l_id]  - 2* Av_Dl[l_id-1] + Av_Dl[l_id-2] 
    num/den
end

Write the amplitude¶

Four vertex amplitudes

Summing over the bulk spin with face amplitude $2j_{\mathrm{bulk}} + 1$.

sl2cfoam-next distributes the edge amplitudes in the vertex amplitudes.

Fixing some parameters¶

  • The Immirzi parameter $\gamma =1$
  • Truncation parameter $\Delta l = 5$
  • Simmetric setting (simplifying assumption)
    1. All the boundary spins equal ($j_f = 1$)
    2. All the boundary intertwiners equal ($i_b =1$)
  • Consequences:
    1. Bulk intertwiners assume values between $0$ and $2j$ ($0-2$)
    2. Bulk spin assume values between $0$ and $3j$ ($0-3$)
    3. All the vertices are similar
  • Strategy:
    1. Precompute all the vertices we need
    2. Contract the vertices to form the amplitude

Assemble the vertices to compute the amplitude¶

In [15]:
i_b = 1; i_idx = i_b + 1;
D4_amp = 0.0
for j_bulk = j_bulk_min:j_bulk_max
    spins = [j, j, j, j, j, j, j, j_bulk, j, j]
    Av = vertex_load(spins,Dl).a
    D4_partial_amp = 0.0
    D = size(Av[i_idx,:,:,i_idx,i_idx])[1]
    for i1 in 1:D, i2 in 1:D, i3 in 1:D, i4 in 1:D
        @inbounds D4_partial_amp += Av[i_idx,i2,i1,i_idx,i_idx]*
                                    Av[i_idx,i1,i4,i_idx,i_idx]*
                                    Av[i_idx,i4,i3,i_idx,i_idx]*
                                    Av[i_idx,i3,i2,i_idx,i_idx]     
    end     
    D4_amp += D4_partial_amp  * (2j_bulk + 1)
end
@show D4_amp;
